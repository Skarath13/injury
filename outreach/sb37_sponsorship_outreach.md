## Compliance Guardrails

- Do not describe the arrangement as referrals, sold leads, exclusive leads, guaranteed cases, or pay-per-case.
- Do not promise lead volume, lead quality, case value, conversion rate, settlement size, or geographic exclusivity.
- Do not use consumer-facing firm intake forms if the form requires you to represent that you were injured or are seeking legal help. Use published email, general contact routes, LinkedIn, or phone instead.
- Any live sponsorship should identify the responsible California law firm or attorney and office location, and should avoid guarantees, quick-cash language, or predictions.
- Keep the offer framed as exploratory until written terms and attorney review are complete.
- Include standard cold-email basics: accurate sender identity, real contact information, and an easy opt-out line if sending in bulk.
